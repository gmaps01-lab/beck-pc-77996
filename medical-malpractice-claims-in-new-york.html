Frequently Asked Questions About Medical Malpractice Claims

How does Beck, P.C. charge for handling medical malpractice cases in New York?
At Beck, P.C., we handle medical malpractice cases on a contingency fee basis, which means you do not pay any upfront legal fees. Our fee is a percentage of the compensation we recover for you, in accordance with New York State law. If your case is not successful, you owe us nothing for our legal services. We will always discuss the exact fee structure and any potential costs at your initial consultation.
How long does it usually take to resolve a medical malpractice claim in New York City?
The timeline for a medical malpractice claim in New York City can vary. On average, cases take between 18 months to 3 years to resolve, depending on factors like the complexity of the case, the willingness of the parties to settle, and the court’s schedule. Beck, P.C. will keep you updated throughout the process and work diligently to resolve your case as efficiently as possible while protecting your interests.
Are your attorneys licensed and experienced in handling New York medical malpractice cases?
Yes, all attorneys at Beck, P.C. are fully licensed to practice law in New York State and have extensive experience handling medical malpractice claims in New York City and the surrounding areas. Our team is well-versed in New York’s specific medical malpractice laws and has a strong track record of representing clients in these complex cases.
What is the process for starting a medical malpractice claim with Beck, P.C.?
